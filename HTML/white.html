Ravindra Jadeja, born on December 6, 1988, in Navagam Ghed, Gujarat, is one of India's most celebrated all-rounders in cricket. Known for his left-arm spin, sharp fielding, and dependable batting, Jadeja began his domestic career with Saurashtra in 2006 and quickly rose through the ranks. He made his international debut in 2009 in both ODIs and T20Is, and later in Tests in 2012. Over the years, he has become a vital part of the Indian team, contributing with both bat and ball across formats. Jadeja is renowned for his consistency, having scored over 4,000 runs and taken more than 250 wickets in Test cricket. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), he has played for teams like Rajasthan Royals, Kochi Tuskers Kerala, and most notably Chennai Super Kings, with whom he won multiple titles. Off the field, he is married to Rivaba Jadeja and is admired for his calm demeanor and fierce competitiveness. His journey from a small town in Gujarat to becoming a cornerstone of Indian cricket is a testament to his talent, resilience, and dedication.

Popular Hero For South India

Thalapathy Vijay, born Joseph Vijay Chandrasekhar on June 22, 1974, in Chennai, is one of the most celebrated actors in Tamil cinema. Son of filmmaker S.A. Chandrasekhar and singer Shoba Chandrasekhar, Vijay began his career as a child artist before making his lead debut in Naalaiya Theerpu (1992). Over the years, he rose to superstardom with blockbuster hits like Ghilli, Thuppakki, Mersal, and Master, earning the affectionate title “Thalapathy” (commander) from fans. Known for his charismatic screen presence, action-packed roles, and socially conscious themes, Vijay has also showcased his musical talent by singing in several of his films. In 2024, he made a significant leap into politics by founding the Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam party, signaling his commitment to public service and youth empowerment. With a massive fan base and a career spanning over three decades, Vijay continues to be a dominant force both on screen and in Tamil Nadu evolving political landscape.

Popular Heroine For South India

Samantha was born on April 28, 1987, in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. She pursued a degree in commerce at Stella Maris College, where she also began modeling. Her acting debut came with the Telugu romantic drama Ye Maaya Chesave (2010), which earned her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut – South. She quickly rose to prominence with acclaimed performances in films like Eega, Neethaane En Ponvasantham, Theri, Mahanati, and Oh! Baby. Her roles often blend emotional depth with strong female characters, earning her multiple awards including four Filmfare Awards South, two Nandi Awards, and a Tamil Nadu State Film Award.
